LIN MANUEL-MIRANDA

Lin Manuel Miranda, a man born on January 16, 1980 in New York, America, is of Puerto Rican descent. His mother, Luz Towns-Miranda, is a psychologist while his father, Luis A. Miranda Jr., is a political consultant. Some might say that Miranda became a lyrical genius due to the fact that he majored in theatre in Wesleyan University, while others might say it is just pure talent.


His first ever musical that boomed was In The Heights , which talks about the issues that Latin and Caribbean people have whilst staying in America. He was only 19 years old when he wrote this musical, which goes on to show just how talented he is. Hamilton , a musical about Alexander Hamilton and the American Revolution, is his most popular musical, with its most streamed track being its opening number with almost 300 million streams on Spotify. Tick tick BOOM! , a musical he directed, had blown up during the pandemic, as well.


Lin Manuel-Miranda is also the song composer of famous Disney movies such as Moana and Encanto . Along with that, he has also acted in some shows such as Brooklyn 99 , where he was Amy Santiago’s brother, Mary Poppins Returns , where he was Jack, one of the main characters of the movie. Recently, he was chosen to be cast as Hermes in the new Percy Jackson series.


The musical that sparked his interest when he was young was Les Misérables, a book turned into a musical about the inequality of 1815 Paris.


Due to his lyrical brilliance along with the fact that Filipinos tend to be musically talented, Lin Manuel-Miranda had been mistaken as Filipino multiple times by the community. With that though, he has told an interviewer that he is flattered to be mistaken as one.


Lin Manuel-Miranda was able to win the EGOT award, which is short for Emmy, Grammy, Oscar, and Tony. So far, only 19 people have won the same status.
